Palm Beach County has experienced the most significant residential price appreciation in South Florida over the past five years. A combination of domestic migration, remote work flexibility, and Florida's tax environment has brought a sustained wave of buyers from the Northeast and Midwest, reshaping the market at every price point.

The Palm Beach County Market

Palm Beach County spans from the urban density of West Palm Beach to the ultra-luxury enclaves of Palm Beach Island and Jupiter Island. It is not a single market — it is a collection of distinct submarkets with different buyer profiles, price points, and long-term dynamics.

What the county shares is demand. Inventory has not kept pace with the buyer pool that arrived post-2020, and prices have adjusted upward accordingly. Buyers who purchased in West Palm Beach or Delray Beach in 2019 have seen significant equity gains. Buyers entering now face a more competitive, higher-priced market than their predecessors.

Palm Beach County Submarkets

West Palm Beach is the county seat and its most urban market. The downtown core — particularly Clematis Street and Rosemary Square — has seen significant investment in retail and residential development. The SoSo (South of Southern) neighborhood is among the most in-demand submarkets in the county for single-family buyers. Median home prices in desirable West Palm Beach neighborhoods range from $500,000 to $2M+.

Palm Beach (the island) is one of the most expensive zip codes in the United States. Oceanfront and lakefront estates along South Ocean Boulevard and North County Road regularly transact above $20M. Seasonal ownership and limited primary residence use characterize much of the market.

Delray Beach has built a reputation as one of Florida's most livable cities. The Atlantic Avenue corridor is one of the most successful pedestrian retail environments in the state. A mix of older single-family homes and new condo development attracts both retirees and younger buyers. Median prices range from $450,000 to $1.5M for most residential properties.

Boca Raton offers strong schools, a well-established luxury market, and consistent demand. The Royal Palm Yacht and Country Club and the Boca West Country Club anchor two of the most active luxury submarkets in the county. The broader Boca market serves a wide range of buyers, from first-time buyers in eastern Boca to estate buyers in Bocaire and The Oaks.

Jupiter and Palm Beach Gardens attract buyers seeking a more suburban pace with strong golf and boating infrastructure. Both cities have grown significantly and now command prices that would have been considered premium even five years ago.

Understanding Palm Beach County's Tax Environment

Palm Beach County's millage rate varies by municipality. West Palm Beach runs approximately 2.1–2.5% of assessed value. Unincorporated Palm Beach County is slightly lower. Florida's Homestead Exemption reduces assessed value by up to $50,000 for primary residents — file with the Palm Beach County Property Appraiser's office by March 1 following your purchase.

Florida's Save Our Homes assessment cap limits annual property tax increases on homesteaded properties to 3% or the Consumer Price Index, whichever is lower. For buyers planning to use a property as a primary residence long-term, this cap provides meaningful protection against rapidly escalating tax bills.

What to Know Before Buying in Palm Beach County

The pace of the Palm Beach County market requires preparation. Pre-approval is a baseline. In the most competitive submarkets, well-priced properties receive offers within days of listing.

Flood zone classification matters across all of Palm Beach County, particularly in coastal communities and near the Intracoastal. Request a flood zone determination before making an offer and get insurance quotes early — the cost varies significantly by property location and structure.

For buyers considering new construction — which is active in several Palm Beach County submarkets — understand that builder contracts are not the same as FAR/BAR contracts. Builder contracts typically favor the builder. Have a real estate attorney review any new construction purchase agreement before signing.
