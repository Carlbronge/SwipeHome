Swipe Home, LLC – Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how your personal information is collected, used, and shared when you visit swipehome.ai (the "Site") or use the Swipe Home mobile application (the "App").

Personal Information We Collect

When you visit the Site or use the App, we automatically collect certain information about your device, including information about your web browser, IP address, time zone, and some of the cookies that are installed on your device. Additionally, as you browse the Site and use the App, we collect information about the individual web pages or products that you view, what websites or search terms referred you to the Site, and information about how you interact with the Site and the App. We refer to this automatically-collected information as "Device Information". We collect Device Information in order to properly provide the services on this Site and utilize analytics to optimize the Site and the App.

We collect Device Information using the following technologies:

  • "Cookies" are data files that are placed on your device or computer and often include an anonymous unique identifier for the user. For more information about cookies, and how to disable cookies, visit http://www.allaboutcookies.org.
  • "Log files" are records that track actions occurring on the Site and the App, and collect data including your IP address, browser type, Internet service provider, referring/exit pages, search terms, pages and products visited, date/time stamps, and how you otherwise interact with the Site and the App.
  • "Web beacons", "tags", and "pixels" are electronic files used to record information about how you browse the Site and the App.

Additionally, when you make a purchase or attempt to make a purchase through the Site or the App, we may collect certain information from you, including your name, financial background, address, payment information (including credit card numbers, PayPal, eChecks, or other), email address, and phone number. We refer to this information as "Customer Information". We collect Customer Information in order to share relevant data with industry vendors and associates in compliance with our Terms and Conditions.

When we talk about "Personal Information" in this Privacy Policy, we are talking both about Device Information and Customer Information.

How Do We Use Your Personal Information?

We use the Customer Information that we collect to fulfill any requests placed through the Site and the App. Additionally, Personal Information is used to:

  • Communicate with you for customer support;
  • Screen our requests for potential risk or fraud;
  • Customize and improve our products and services; and
  • When in line with the preferences you have shared with us, provide you with information or marketing material for our products or services.

All of this Personal Information is used in compliance with the Terms and Conditions agreed to by each user of the Site and the App.

We use the Device Information that we collect to help us screen for potential risk and fraud (in particular, your IP address), and more generally to improve and optimize the Site and the App (for example, by generating analytics about how our customers browse and interact with the Site and the App, and to assess the success of our marketing and advertising campaigns).

Sharing Your Personal Information

We work with other service providers and third-party companies to provide the services on the Site and the App. We share your Personal Information with third parties to help us use your Personal Information, as described above. The Site aims to work with trusted partners and organizations that are also bound by state and federal laws governing privacy and security; however, we encourage users to be aware of our partners and review any privacy policy made available by those companies.

For example, we use national and state MLS services to power our database for the Site and the App — you can read more about how MLS uses your Personal Information here: https://www.mls.com/Home/PrivacyPolicy.mvc. We also use Google Analytics to help understand how customers use the Site and the App — read more about how Google uses your Personal Information here: https://www.google.com/intl/en/policies/privacy/. You can also opt-out of Google Analytics here: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.

Finally, we may also share your Personal Information to comply with applicable laws and regulations, to respond to a subpoena, search warrant or other lawful request for information we receive, or to otherwise protect our rights. We also reserve the right to share Personal Information with your consent or as otherwise disclosed during the process of data collection and sharing.

We are compliant with the GDPR and have incorporated and adopted the standard contractual clauses adopted by the European Commission regarding Personal Information transfers as security for the transfer of any of your information.

  • The right to obtain access to the Personal Information we have collected;
  • The right to correct or rectify any inaccurate or incomplete Personal Information;
  • The right to be forgotten and request the deletion of any Personal Information absent a compelling reason to keep it;
  • The right to restrict and limit the processing of stored Personal Information;
  • The right to request and receive your Personal Information in a readable format in order to transmit it to another controller;
  • The right to object to the processing of your Personal Information for some circumstances other than a contractual or legal obligation;
  • The right to not be subject to an automated processing system that includes profiling;
  • The right to file a complaint with the relevant national data protection regulator regarding the way we have handled Personal Information; and
  • The right to withdraw consent to use such Personal Information, including its use for marketing purposes.

California Privacy Policy

Pursuant to the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 ("CCPA"), this California Privacy Policy provides California residents with a description of our practices regarding the collection, use, sale, and disclosure of their Personal Information. Under the CCPA, "Personal Information" is information that identifies, relates to, describes, or could reasonably be associated or linked with a particular California resident or household. This California Privacy Policy does not apply to our job applicants, employees, contractors, owners, directors, or officers where the Personal Information we collect about those individuals relates to their current, former, or potential role with us.

We intend to collect the following categories of Personal Information as listed in the CCPA:

  • Identifiers. Such as name, contact information, IP address, social media username, and online identifiers.
  • Customer Records Information. Personal information as defined in the California customer records law, such as name, contact information, and financial account information.
  • Protected Class Information. Characteristics of protected classifications under California or federal law, such as gender and age.
  • Transaction Information. Commercial information, such as order history, shopping cart contents, payment details, and preferences.
  • Online Use Information. Internet or network activity information, such as browsing history, search history, and interactions with our online properties or ads.
  • Geolocation Information. Such as device location and approximate location derived from IP address.
  • Audio/Video Information. Audio, electronic, visual, and similar information, such as call and video recordings.
  • Inferences. Inferences drawn from any of the Personal Information listed above to create a profile about an individual's preferences and characteristics.

Your rights under the CCPA include:

  • Right to request the information, including each category of Personal Information, that we have collected in the preceding 12 months;
  • Right to request that we delete such Personal Information collected from you;
  • Right to request to opt out of a sale of Personal Information; and
  • Right to be free from unlawful discriminatory treatment for exercising your rights under the CCPA.

If you are a California resident, you may request certain information once per year about our disclosure of certain categories of Personal Information during the preceding calendar year to third parties for such third parties' own direct marketing purposes. Requests may be made to the contact information below.
